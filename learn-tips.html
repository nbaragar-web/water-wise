Water Conservation Tips

Practical advice for saving water in every area of your life

Daily Water-Saving Habits

Small changes in daily routines can lead to significant water savings over time. Here are actionable tips organized by area of your home and daily activities.

Kitchen Tips

Dishwashing

  • Always run full loads in the dishwasher—it uses less water than hand washing even when full
  • Scrape food off plates instead of rinsing before loading (saves 20 gallons per load)
  • If hand washing, use two basins: one for washing, one for rinsing
  • Don't let water run continuously while washing dishes
  • Soak pots and pans instead of letting water run while scrubbing
  • Use the "light" or "eco" cycle on your dishwasher when possible

Food Preparation

  • Wash vegetables in a pan of water instead of under running water
  • Reuse pasta or vegetable steaming water for soup stocks or watering plants
  • Keep a pitcher of water in the fridge instead of running tap until cold
  • Defrost frozen foods in the refrigerator overnight instead of under running water
  • Compost food scraps instead of using garbage disposal (saves water and benefits garden)
  • Use minimal water when boiling—just enough to cover food

Drinking Water

  • Serve water only when requested at meals
  • Use a reusable water bottle and fill once daily
  • Install a water filter instead of buying bottled water
  • Use leftover drinking water for plants rather than pouring down drain

Bathroom Tips

Showering

  • Limit showers to 5 minutes (set a timer or play a 5-minute playlist)
  • Turn water off while soaping up or shampooing
  • Place a bucket in shower to catch water while it warms up
  • Take showers instead of baths (baths use 70 gallons vs. 25-gallon shower)
  • Install a low-flow showerhead (reduces flow from 5 to 2 gallons per minute)
  • Consider "navy showers": wet down, turn off water, soap up, rinse off

Sink and Faucet Use

  • Turn off water while brushing teeth (saves 8 gallons per day)
  • Turn off water while lathering hands with soap
  • Turn off water while shaving—fill basin with small amount instead
  • Use a cup of water for mouth rinsing instead of running tap
  • Install aerators on all faucets (reduces flow by 30-50%)

Toilet Tips

  • Don't use toilet as a wastebasket for tissues, insects, or trash
  • Fix running toilets immediately (test with food coloring in tank)
  • Install a toilet tank bank or fill bottle to reduce water per flush
  • Consider dual-flush toilet conversion kit
  • Never flush medications—take to proper disposal sites
  • If it's yellow, let it mellow; if it's brown, flush it down (controversial but effective)

Laundry Tips

  • Wash only full loads—or adjust water level for smaller loads
  • Use cold water whenever possible (90% of washing machine energy goes to heating water)
  • Pre-treat stains instead of washing twice
  • Reuse lightly worn clothes instead of washing after every wear
  • Use high-spin or extended spin cycle to remove more water (reduces dryer time too)
  • Upgrade to ENERGY STAR washing machine (uses 35% less water)
  • Direct washing machine drain to landscape (greywater system where legal)

Outdoor and Garden Tips

Lawn Care

  • Water deeply but infrequently—1 inch per week is sufficient for most lawns
  • Water between 4-10am when evaporation is lowest
  • Set mower blade to 3 inches—taller grass retains moisture better
  • Leave grass clippings on lawn as natural mulch
  • Don't water during rain or if rain is forecasted within 24 hours
  • Let lawn go dormant in summer—it will revive with fall rains
  • Aerate lawn to improve water absorption

Garden and Landscaping

  • Apply 2-4 inches of mulch around plants to reduce evaporation
  • Plant drought-resistant native plants and succulents
  • Group plants with similar water needs together
  • Use soaker hoses or drip irrigation instead of sprinklers
  • Direct downspouts toward planted areas
  • Create swales and berms to direct water to plants
  • Remove weeds promptly—they compete for water
  • Use rain barrels to collect roof runoff (1 inch of rain on 1,000 sq ft = 600 gallons)

Trees and Shrubs

  • Water trees slowly at drip line (outer edge of canopy)
  • Deep water trees monthly during dry periods
  • Mulch around trees and shrubs to retain moisture
  • Plant in fall when water needs are lower
  • Choose native species adapted to local rainfall

Seasonal Water-Saving Tips

Spring

  • Check irrigation system for winter damage and leaks
  • Adjust sprinkler heads before starting season
  • Clean gutters to maximize rainwater collection
  • Apply pre-emergent to reduce weeds (less water competition)
  • Overseed lawn to fill in bare spots

Summer

  • Water early morning to minimize evaporation
  • Adjust irrigation schedule based on rainfall
  • Use pool cover to reduce evaporation
  • Reduce lawn watering frequency—let grass go dormant
  • Wash car on lawn to dual-purpose water use

Fall

  • Reduce watering frequency as temperatures drop
  • Winterize irrigation system before first freeze
  • Plant bulbs and perennials to establish before winter
  • Apply thick mulch layer to protect plants and retain moisture
  • Disconnect and drain outdoor hoses

Winter

  • Insulate outdoor pipes to prevent freezing and bursting
  • Check for leaks from heating system
  • Monitor indoor humidity—overly dry air increases water use
  • Plan next year's water-wise landscaping
  • Repair and maintain irrigation equipment

Special Circumstances

During Drought or Water Restrictions

  • Prioritize watering trees over lawn—trees are expensive to replace
  • Stop watering lawn entirely—it will recover
  • Reduce showering frequency or duration
  • Use paper plates temporarily to reduce dishwashing
  • Reuse greywater for outdoor watering where allowed
  • Cover pool to prevent evaporation loss

For Renters

  • Request landlord fix leaks (document to show higher water bills)
  • Install temporary low-flow showerheads and aerators (take with you)
  • Use behavioral changes that don't require equipment
  • Place brick or bottle in toilet tank to reduce flush volume
  • Talk to landlord about water-efficient upgrades

For Apartments

  • Report building leaks to management
  • Organize tenant conservation challenges
  • Request water-efficient fixtures in common areas
  • Container garden on balcony using drip irrigation
  • Advocate for building-wide conservation measures

Family and Education Tips

Teaching Children

  • Make it a game: time showers and reward improvements
  • Post reminder signs near faucets
  • Let kids track family water savings on a chart
  • Explain where water comes from and why it's precious
  • Involve kids in fixing leaks and installing aerators
  • Use visual aids (fill containers to show gallons saved)

Household Challenges

  • Set family water reduction goals with rewards
  • Compare monthly water bills to track progress
  • Assign each family member a conservation task
  • Calculate money saved and allocate toward fun activity
  • Share successes with friends and neighbors

The 80-20 Rule

Focus on these high-impact changes for maximum water savings with minimum effort:

  1. Fix all leaks (10-20% savings)
  2. Install low-flow showerheads (15-20% savings)
  3. Run full dishwasher loads only (5-10% savings)
  4. Reduce outdoor watering (20-30% savings)
  5. Shorter showers (10-15% savings)

Total potential savings: 60-95%

Advanced Tips for Maximum Conservation

  • Install whole-house water filtration to reuse greywater
  • Replace traditional lawn with native plants or xeriscaping
  • Install composting toilet for zero-water waste disposal
  • Collect and store air conditioning condensate
  • Install rainwater harvesting system with large storage capacity
  • Use soil moisture sensors connected to smart irrigation
  • Pressure-wash driveway only when absolutely necessary

Technology and Tools

  • Smart shower devices: Track usage and provide feedback in real-time
  • Leak detection systems: Alert you immediately when leaks occur
  • Smart irrigation controllers: Adjust watering based on weather and soil moisture
  • Water usage apps: Track consumption and set conservation goals
  • Flow meters: Monitor usage by fixture to identify waste sources

Making It Stick

To turn water conservation into lasting habits:

  • Start with 3-5 easy changes and build from there
  • Post reminders near water fixtures
  • Track your progress and celebrate successes
  • Share your journey with friends and family
  • Review water bills monthly to stay motivated
  • Join online conservation communities for support
  • Remember: every drop counts toward a sustainable future
